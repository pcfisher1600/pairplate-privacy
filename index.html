PairPlate

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 17, 2026

PairPlate doesn't collect, transmit, or share personal data. Your taste profile, ratings, plans, pantry, and settings live only on your device. We don't have servers, accounts, or user databases. The only outbound network traffic happens if you choose to enable LLM-backed features by entering your own Anthropic API key — in which case your requests go directly from your device to Anthropic's API, not through us.

What data PairPlate handles

All of the following is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. None of it leaves your device unless you explicitly enable an LLM feature with your own API key (see Optional LLM features below).

  • Onboarding choices: zip code, household size, dietary restrictions, household members and their dislikes, time-of-week preference.
  • Taste profile: your answers to "would you rather cook A or B?" pairs, used to build a preference fingerprint.
  • Generated plans, carts, and shopping lists.
  • Cooking history: which recipes you started, finished, abandoned, and rated.
  • Pantry: ingredients auto-stocked from submitted carts and from receipt scans, with their shelf-life timestamps.
  • Recipe library: includes the bundled seed corpus plus any recipes you create or import.
  • App-internal event log: timestamps of in-app actions used to compute the on-device analytics dashboard.

We do not collect:

  • Real names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any other contact information.
  • Device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV) for tracking.
  • Location beyond the zip code you enter.
  • Health, financial, or biometric data.
  • Photos, videos, or any other media beyond what you explicitly capture for the in-app vision features (see below).

Camera and photo library

Two app features ask for permission to use the camera or the photo library:

  • Fridge-finder: photograph the inside of your fridge so the app can identify ingredients and suggest recipes.
  • Receipt parser: photograph a grocery receipt so the app can extract line items and stock your pantry.

Photos are used only to make a single request to the LLM service you've configured (see below) and are not retained on disk by PairPlate after the request completes. We do not upload photos to any server we control. We do not have any server.

You can revoke camera or photo-library access at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy → Camera / Photos → PairPlate.

Optional LLM features (Anthropic API)

PairPlate can use Anthropic's Claude API to power optional features: explainers, substitutions, the cooking-mode coach, novel-recipe generation, recipe imports, fridge identification, and receipt parsing.

These features are off by default. To enable them, you paste your own Anthropic API key into Settings → Cooking coach. The key is stored in your device's iOS Keychain and is never transmitted to any server we control.

When an LLM feature is invoked, your device sends a request directly to api.anthropic.com. The request includes:

  • The system + user prompt for that feature.
  • The relevant context (e.g., the recipe step, the pasted recipe text, the fridge photo, the receipt photo).
  • Your API key in the x-api-key header.

These requests are subject to Anthropic's privacy policy, available at anthropic.com/legal/privacy. We have no visibility into them.

If you remove your API key (Settings → Cooking coach → Remove), all LLM features fall back to deterministic on-device stubs and no further outbound requests are made.

Analytics, advertising, and tracking

PairPlate contains:

  • No third-party analytics SDKs (no Firebase, Mixpanel, Amplitude, etc.).
  • No advertising SDKs.
  • No tracking as defined by Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework.

The app's privacy manifest (PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy) declares no collected data types and no tracking domains.

The on-device "Dashboard" view computes summary metrics (plan approval rate, cooking completion rate, etc.) from your local event log. These metrics never leave the device.

Crash reporting

PairPlate uses Apple's built-in MetricKit framework for crash reporting. MetricKit deliveries are processed by iOS and may be shared with Apple under their standard developer crash-reporting flow if you have Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements → Share with App Developers enabled. We do not run any third-party crash-reporting service (no Sentry, Crashlytics, etc.).

Children

PairPlate is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children. The app does not require an account, has no social features, and contains no in-app purchases or advertising.

Data deletion

Because all data lives only on your device, you can delete everything PairPlate knows about you by deleting the app. There is no separate deletion request to file with us, because we don't hold the data.

Changes to this policy

If this policy materially changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top and, where reasonable, surface the change in-app on the next update.
